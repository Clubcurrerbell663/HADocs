## 📝 How to use HADocs

The tool performs several tasks to manage your data. 

### Generating Documentation
The tool reads your Home Assistant configuration files. It creates a document that lists every device, entity, and automation. You can review this list to check your system setup. It updates whenever you run the scan.

### Checking Health
The Health Score feature analyzes your configuration. It looks for conflicts or weak spots. It provides a numerical score to show how well your system operates. A high score means your configuration follows standard practices. A low score suggests parts for you to fix.

### Finding Unused Entities
Your Home Assistant setup often gathers many entities over time. Some entities become part of old integrations or devices you no longer use. HADocs identifies these inactive items. You can remove them to clear your database. This keeps your system lean and fast.

### Visualizing Architecture
The tool creates maps of your smart home. It shows how devices connect to integrations. You see a clear diagram of your current structure. This helps when you plan new automations or expand your smart home.

## 🛠️ Common Tasks

### Scanning your system
Open the application on your desktop. Enter your Home Assistant address and your long-lived access token. Click the scan button. The tool fetches your files and generates the report. This process takes a few minutes depending on your system size.

### Saving reports
The tool saves your data in a local folder on your computer. You choose the location during the initial scan. Open these files with any web browser to view your reports.

### Updating configurations
If you change your Home Assistant settings, run a new scan. The tool overwrites the old report with the new data. This ensures your documentation stays accurate.

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need to be a programmer to use this?
No. The interface works like any standard Windows application. You enter information and click buttons to see results.

### Does HADocs send my data to the internet?
No. The application runs locally on your computer. Only your computer connects to your Home Assistant instance. Your data stays on your machine at all times.

### Can I share the documentation with others?
Yes. The generated reports are standard files. You can email them or save them to a shared drive for other household members to see.

### What happens if the scan fails?
Check your Home Assistant address. Make sure the long-lived access token is valid. Ensure your computer has a stable connection to your home network.

## 🔐 Security
Your security remains a priority. The tool uses industry-standard encryption for your connection token. It does not store passwords. You keep full control over your access settings.
